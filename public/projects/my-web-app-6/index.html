Project Overview#

This project involves the development of a product prototype designed to solve a significant real-life issue: decision fatigue following a demanding workday. This mental barrier often prevents individuals from utilizing available ingredients, leading to impulsive, expensive “panic orders” even when their pantry is fully stocked. The prototype, PantryPro, addresses this problem by focusing on minimizing cognitive load through several strategic design pillars:

  • Tailored Recipe Recommendations: To eliminate the stress of browsing through endless options, the app provides a single, high-probability suggestion.
  • Energy-Level Integration: The interface features time-based labels—such as “Quick (15 min)” and “Creative (45+ min)"—to ensure suggested meals align with the user’s current mental and physical capacity.
  • Reduced Input Friction: The project prioritizes ease of use by implementing “Quick-Add Tags” for common items like eggs and pasta, allowing users to input their inventory with a single tap rather than manual typing. By prioritizing user-centric functionality over technical complexity, the prototype transforms a stressful chores into a streamlined, minutes-long experience that encourages healthier eating and reduces food waste.
